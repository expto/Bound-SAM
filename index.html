Bound-SAM: Lightweight SAM model for Boundary-aware Skin Lesion Segmentation

Abstract

Skin lesion segmentation aims to automatically delineate lesion regions in skin‐cancer images. It plays crucial role in clinical applications, and thus has obtain much attention from both medical practitioners and AI researchers. Recently, transformer-based methods have achieved notable progress owing to their strong global modeling capacity. However, in real-world skin lesion images, low lesion–skin contrast, vascular interference, and hair occlusion frequently yield ambiguous boundaries, making accurate segmentation nontrivial. To overcome these limitations, we propose a novel \textbf{Bound}ary-aware \textbf{SAM}, called Bound-SAM, for skin lesion segmentation. Bound-SAM builds on the pre-trained SAM backbone and introduces two parameter-free modules, Multi-scale Boundary Guidance (MB) and Mask-Guided Contrastive (MC), to enhance SAM’s ability to mitigate the ambiguity of lesion boundaries. Specifically, MB constructs multi-scale boundary supervision that guides shallow encoder layers to capture coarse edges and deeper layers to model fine boundaries, thereby enhancing overall boundary sensitivity. MC employs contrastive learning to amplify feature differences between lesions and healthy skin, thereby enhancing foreground–background discriminability. We conduct extensive experiments on two publicly available datasets, ISIC-2017 and ISIC-2018, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The source code is available at https://github.com/expto/Bound-SAM/.
